# Ouija AIYES Submission Brief

## Track

Track 1 - AI Application Development.

## One-Liner

Ouija is a student-only AI experiment interpreter that helps middle and high school students understand science labs, compare their table data with expected patterns, and build their own evidence-backed claims without generating a full lab report.

## Problem

Students often get stuck between raw lab instructions, messy data tables, and a blank conclusion paragraph. Generic chatbots can answer too much and turn into report writers. Static references explain concepts but do not look at the student's actual data.

## Solution

Ouija connects four pieces in one workflow:

- Experiment classification from a student description.
- Grounded expected-results explanation with visible citations.
- Judge Demo Path for a five-step evaluator walkthrough.
- AI Runtime Proof for fallback/web-search readiness, live evaluation coverage, server-only secret boundaries, and MCP bridge mode.
- Model Strategy that exposes candidate ranking, matched signals, fallback logic, validators, and risk controls.
- Technical Depth Proof that condenses decision trace, evaluation harness, grounding quality, expected-pattern engine, privacy, and integrity into one judge-readable panel.
- AI Evaluation Harness that scores classifier confidence, coverage, grounding, validators, safety/integrity, and fallback boundaries.
- Data Handling Ledger that exposes privacy score, data flows, retention, student controls, and server-only API-key boundaries.
- AIYES Rubric Fit that maps every run to the official problem/relevance, AI technical design/model strategy, and UX/design criteria.
- Pre-Lab Design Coach that turns the match into variables, controls, repeats, table columns, a source task, safety gate, and blank hypothesis starter before data collection.
- Learning Impact Loop that scores student readiness and shows outcome, data-quality, concept-learning, integrity, pattern-evidence, repeat-reliability, and next-trial metrics.
- Learning Exit Ticket that asks students to explain variables, graph pattern, and next step themselves before writing.
- Student Level Lens that adapts the same run for middle-school pattern reading or high-school quantitative reasoning, controls, repeats, and uncertainty.
- Concept Mastery Check that scores whether the student understands the independent variable, expected evidence pattern, and integrity boundary before evidence export.
- Student Reflection Workspace that captures student-authored exit-ticket drafts and flags empty or too-short answers for revision.
- Progress Portfolio that converts saved runs into score trend, subject breadth, strongest-run, milestone, and next-action evidence.
- Portfolio Story Builder that turns saved-run evidence into prompts and blanks for a student-authored progress story.
- MCP Integration Coach that validates a Composio Search source audit, Composio Scholar claim check, plus handoffs to Google Docs, Google Sheets, Google Drive, Google Classroom, Google Forms, Google Calendar, and Notion through a consent-gated server dry-run while keeping credentials server-side.
- Guided Lab Flow for the student's current next action and six workflow stages.
- Grounding Audit for citation visibility, source agreement, mixed-evidence boundaries, and a student source task.
- Spreadsheet/CSV paste import, editable table data, graphing, expected-pattern overlay, and pattern checks.
- Method Audit for variables, controls, assumptions, confounds, and reproducibility.
- Pattern Evidence Engine for whole-table trend, peak, and ratio support before the student writes a claim.
- Reliability Coach for repeated-trial counts, averages, spread, and retest recommendations.
- Safety Coach for PPE, material limits, cleanup, stop conditions, and adult-review boundaries.
- Concept Coach for vocabulary, explanation steps, source tasks, and misconception checks.
- Next Trial Planner that suggests whether to extend the pattern or repeat/fix a flagged measurement.
- Reasoning Trail that exposes the AI pipeline and maps each run to Track 1 evidence.
- Evaluation Bench that runs nine live checks against the analysis engine.
- AI Model Card that summarizes architecture, grounding, evaluation, privacy, and safeguards.
- Saved Labs plus Progress Portfolio for browser-local snapshots and repeated learning evidence across runs.
- Evidence Packet for a copyable source-backed handoff that keeps the final conclusion student-written.
- Judge Brief for a compact Track 1 proof surface during live evaluation, including hosted deck/video/source links and an AIYES submission checklist.
- Low-confidence boundary handling for unsupported experiment descriptions.
- Custom Lab Triage for unsupported experiments: inferred focus, starter table columns, a Custom Investigation Planner with variables, controls, repeat guidance, starter rows, hypothesis blanks, source-search queries, clarifying questions, and a teacher-confirmation boundary.
- Claim Coach output that shows evidence gaps and Socratic next questions without writing the conclusion.

## Supported Demo Labs

- Physics: projectile motion, pendulum period vs length, and Ohm's law circuits.
- Chemistry: reaction rate vs temperature and density layering.
- Biology: enzyme activity vs temperature and plant growth vs light color.
- Earth science: water filtration and turbidity.

## Differentiator

Ouija is not a homework chatbot. The product is designed around student reasoning: it classifies the experiment, helps the student plan variables/controls/repeats/sources/safety before data collection, gives a guided next action, overlays expected pattern values on the graph, checks classroom safety boundaries, checks data quality, scores whole-pattern evidence, checks repeated-trial reliability, teaches the vocabulary and misconception traps, shows sources, gives unsupported labs a safe triage plan, suggests the next measurement to run, asks the student to explain variables/pattern/next step in a Learning Exit Ticket, adapts the support for middle or high school through Student Level Lens, scores quick concept mastery checks before evidence export, gives the student a workspace to draft those answers themselves, turns saved runs into a Progress Portfolio and student-authored Portfolio Story Builder, validates a Composio/MCP source-audit, Scholar claim-check, and classroom handoff with a readiness matrix and server dry-run, asks the next reasoning question, exports an evidence packet, and leaves the final claim blank for the student to finish.
